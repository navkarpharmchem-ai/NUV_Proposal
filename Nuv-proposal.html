Background & Rationale

In today's competitive placement environment, MBA graduates are evaluated not only on theoretical knowledge but also on aptitude, role-fit, and industry readiness. Companies increasingly expect candidates to demonstrate:

  • Strong aptitude and reasoning abilities
  • Clear alignment of traits and strengths with career roles
  • Practical role-specific knowledge and readiness before hiring

To ensure our MBA students are well-prepared for placements by the beginning of their 3rd semester, we propose a 3-Phase Comprehensive Career Readiness Assessment System. This initiative will enhance student preparedness, strengthen placement outcomes, and position NUV as a pioneer in scientific career readiness.

Proposed 3-Phase System

1
Aptitude, Verbal & Reasoning Assessment
Format: Standardized online test (Quantitative, Verbal, Logical Reasoning).

Purpose: Benchmark students against placement test standards.

Outcome: Identify skill gaps and provide focused training modules.
2
Psychometric Assessment
Framework: Based on global models (Big Five, RIASEC, etc.).

Purpose: Assess personality, interests, and work preferences to determine best-fit career domains (Finance, Marketing, Analytics, HR, Consulting, Operations, etc.).

Outcome: Students receive a career-role recommendation report with fit scores.
3
Role-Specific Readiness Assessment
Example: For a student aligned to Financial Analyst, a 20‚Äì30 question domain test will assess technical, theoretical, and market knowledge.

Purpose: Evaluate current industry readiness level.

Outcome: Each student receives a 10-month personalized action plan covering:
  • Domain knowledge building
  • Relevant certifications
  • Skill development roadmap
  • Practical exposure activities
